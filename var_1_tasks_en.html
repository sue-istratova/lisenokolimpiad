1. (1) The number 11 is "symmetrical", i.e. it is read equally from left to right and from right to left. Write the symmetric number following it.

2. (1-2) The 3x4 board was cut into corners of 3 cells. Give an example of such a partition.

3. (1-2) Write the smallest 3-digit number, all digits of which are different. 4. (1-2-3) Write down all sets of three digits, each of which is equal to 2, 4 or 6, if the order of the digits is unimportant (i.e. sets 224 and 242 are considered the same).

5. (1-2-3-4) Currant, raspberry and strawberry jam were poured into three jars labeled "raspberry", "strawberry" and "raspberry or strawberry" . All the inscriptions turned out to be incorrect. What kind of jam was poured into the strawberry jar?

6. (1-2-3-4) When the teacher scolded Denis for bad grades, he said: "All brilliant people got bad grades at school, so I'm a brilliant person." Is he right?

7. (2-3-4) A candy was stolen from a girl. As you know, those who steal candy are always lying. Her brother said he knew who stole the candy. Is he guilty?

8. (3-4) Five rabbits were put in four cages. Prove that there is a cage in which there are at least two rabbits.

9. (3-4) The watermelon was cut into 3 parts and eaten. It turned out four crusts. How could this be?

10. (4) There are three coins. One of them is fake, which weighs less than the real one. How to determine a counterfeit coin with the help of one weighing.
